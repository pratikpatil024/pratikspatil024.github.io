Journey in Blockchain

Polygon Labs

My primary focus involves developing and optimizing the networking protocol, consensus systems, throughput, and scalability of the Matic PoS chains.
In a recent project, I, along with my colleagues, worked on implementing a solution to process transactions in parallel, effectively increasing throughput.
Additionally, I implemented support for EIP-4337 transactions within the PoS chain.

Carnegie Mellon University

I had the privilege of working on a Blockchain-based project called ZUZ while under the guidance of Professor Seth Goldstein at Carnegie Mellon University (CMU).
My responsibilities included designing the network architecture and developing chaincode (smart contract) using Hyperledger Fabric and NEAR Protocol.
ZUZlab offers a unique platform that enables individuals and businesses to monetize their network reputation.

Tech Mahindra

As a Blockchain Intern with Tech Mahindra's Blockchain Competency Business Unit, I collaborated with a team on a project to develop a platform that simplifies the process of creating a Hyperledger Fabric blockchain network for developers.
Our aim was to provide a user-friendly and a cost-efficient alternative to the "IBM Blockchain Platform".

Tamil Nadu e-Governance

I designed and developed a decentralized Blockchain web application with the purpose of preventing cyber threats and unauthorized manipulations of government websites.
This innovative solution was recognized in the "Tamil Nadu Blockchain Policy 2020" which was released by the Chief Minister of Tamil Nadu.
To achieve this, I utilized my skills in Hyperledger Fabric, NodeJS, and ReactJS.
